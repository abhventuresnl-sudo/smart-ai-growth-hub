Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Map Out What You Want to Automate

Before touching any tools, spend 15 minutes listing the repetitive tasks that eat your time every week. Common candidates for Shopify automation include answering customer questions, writing product descriptions, sending order follow-up emails, recovering abandoned carts, and updating inventory.

Here’s how to do it:

  1. Open a spreadsheet or notepad and write down your top 5 time-consuming tasks
  2. Mark each task as: Customer-Facing, Content, or Operations
  3. Prioritise the tasks that happen most often and take the most time

Pro tip: Focus on automating tasks you currently do manually — not tasks you've never tried. Automation works best when you understand the process first.

Step 2: Set Up AI Customer Support with Tidio

Customer questions are one of the biggest time drains for solo store owners. Tidio's AI can handle common questions — shipping times, return policy, order tracking — automatically, 24/7.

Here’s how to do it:

  1. Go to the Shopify App Store and install Tidio
  2. Connect Tidio to your Shopify store and sync your product catalogue
  3. Enable the Lyro AI bot and set your business hours and fallback message

Pro tip: Write out your 10 most common customer questions and add them to Tidio's FAQ section during setup. The more context you give the AI, the better its responses will be.

Step 3: Automate Email Marketing with Klaviyo AI

Email automation is where most Shopify stores leave money on the table. Klaviyo's AI can automatically send welcome emails, abandoned cart reminders, and post-purchase sequences — all personalised to each customer.

Here’s how to do it:

  1. Install Klaviyo from the Shopify App Store and connect your store
  2. Enable the Abandoned Cart flow (pre-built templates are available)
  3. Turn on the Welcome Series and Post-Purchase follow-up sequences

Pro tip: Don't try to build every flow on day one. Start with Abandoned Cart — it typically recovers 5–15% of lost sales automatically.

Step 4: Use AI to Generate Product Descriptions at Scale

Writing unique, SEO-friendly product descriptions for every item in your store is time-consuming. AI writing tools like Jasper or even ChatGPT can produce a solid first draft in seconds.

Here’s how to do it:

  1. Choose an AI writing tool (Jasper, ChatGPT, or similar)
  2. Create a prompt template that includes: product name, key features, target customer, and tone
  3. Generate descriptions in batches and paste into Shopify — always review before publishing

Pro tip: Include your brand voice in your prompt. For example: 'Write in a friendly, conversational tone for first-time ecommerce shoppers.'

Step 5: Connect It All with Make.com (Optional but Powerful)

Once you have individual automations running, Make.com lets you connect them into full workflows. For example: when a new product is added to Shopify → automatically generate a description with AI → post to social media.

Here’s how to do it:

  1. Create a free Make.com account at make.com
  2. Choose the Shopify module as your trigger (e.g. 'Watch New Products')
  3. Add actions to connect your AI tools and social platforms

Pro tip: Start with one simple scenario in Make before building complex multi-step workflows. A working simple automation beats a broken complex one.

Alternative Approaches

The method above works great for most beginners, but here are some variations worth knowing:

Use Zapier Instead of Make.com

Zapier is slightly easier for beginners but has fewer features on the free plan. Good option if you need something running quickly without a learning curve.

Best for: Absolute beginners who want simple two-step automations

Use Shopify Flow (Built-in)

Shopify's own automation tool is available on paid plans. It handles inventory alerts, order tagging, and customer segmentation natively — no third-party tools needed.

Best for: Store owners who want automation without adding new software

Hire a VA + AI Combo

If full automation feels overwhelming, a virtual assistant using AI tools can handle tasks at a fraction of the cost of a full employee.

Best for: Store owners who prefer human oversight but want to reduce workload

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to automate a Shopify store with AI?

You can start for free with tools like Tidio, Klaviyo (up to 500 contacts), and Make.com (basic plan). A reasonable beginner budget for AI automation is $30–$100/month, which can save many hours of manual work each week.

Do I need to know how to code to automate Shopify?

No coding is required. All the tools in this guide are designed for non-technical users. If you can drag, drop, and fill in a form, you can set up these automations.

Will AI automation make my store feel impersonal?

Not if done correctly. Good AI automation is designed to feel personal — for example, Klaviyo sends emails with the customer's name and references their specific purchase. The goal is to scale personalisation, not remove it.

How long does it take to set up these automations?

Most of the basic automations in this guide can be set up in an afternoon. The email flows in Klaviyo have pre-built templates that take under an hour to configure.

What if an AI tool makes a mistake?

Always review AI-generated content before it goes live — especially product descriptions and email copy. For customer-facing chatbots, set a fallback to hand off to a human when the AI is unsure.

Which automation should I set up first?

Start with Abandoned Cart emails in Klaviyo. It directly recovers revenue you're already losing, and it typically takes less than an hour to activate using Klaviyo's pre-built template.
